## How this list was compiled and how to extend it

This directory was assembled from multiple high-quality sources, each useful for ongoing discovery of economist websites:

- **BREAD membership directory** (https://www.ibread.org/who-we-are) is the single richest source for development economists, with hyperlinked personal websites for every fellow and affiliate. It currently lists 200+ members.
- **RePEc Author Service** (https://ideas.repec.org) indexes **3,385+ economists** with social media and website links, searchable by field (development, labour, econometrics, microeconomics). This is the closest thing to a comprehensive directory.
- **Job market candidate pages** from top PhD programs are the best source for junior researchers. Nearly every JMC maintains a personal website. Particularly rich programs include MIT, Harvard, Chicago, Princeton, Stanford, LSE, Oxford, Bonn, CEMFI, and Barcelona GSE. These pages refresh annually each fall.
- **J-PAL affiliated professors** (https://www.povertyactionlab.org/affiliated-professors) lists 290+ development and applied micro researchers with links to their institutional and personal pages.
- **IZA fellows directory** provides a comprehensive list of labor economists worldwide.
- **GitHub Pages** has become the dominant platform for junior economists' websites — searching `site:github.io` combined with economics terms yields many additional results.
- **Asjad Naqvi's DiD resources page** (https://asjadnaqvi.github.io/DiD/docs/resources) is an excellent curated directory of causal inference researchers and their websites.

The total list above contains **over 150 unique economists** with personal website URLs across the five target subfields. The list skews toward researchers who actively maintain websites with working papers, blog posts, or research updates — making it well-suited for RSS/feed aggregation.
